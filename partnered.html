Phase 1

Immediately after joining, my first priority was to overhaul the free prototype and relaunch it as a paid product at SXSW. We hosted our own event with panelists from our user base and I gave product demos to VIP guests that we targeted in advance.

The relaunch was a success and we had our first paid customers shortly afterwards.

This was an important experience for me! I realized afterwards that after all these years of working at startups, this was my first time closing the first sale for a software product. Until then, I'd only ever worked on apps that either already had paying customers or never would.

Phase 2

After the relaunch, we continuted to iterate on features and I added a research project to get to know our largest customer, an advertising agency. I met their team leader every week for several months to learn about their needs.

What I observed was that it took too much organizational change to incorporate our product into their marketing campaigns and that was hindering a larger company-wide adoption. The solution was to redesign and expand a little-used feature called partnerships, which turned effectively turned it into a vendor database and helped track customer relationships.
